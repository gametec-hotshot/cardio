🫀 How Your Heart Works

A muscular pump the size of your fist. Beats ~100,000 times a day.

The 4 Chambers

①

Right Atrium

Receives deoxygenated blood from the body. It's the waiting room before blood gets sent to the lungs.

②

Right Ventricle

The "lighter" pump — sends blood to nearby lungs to pick up oxygen.

③

Left Atrium

Receives freshly oxygenated blood from the lungs — the "good stuff" your body needs.

④

Left Ventricle

The heart's muscle powerhouse — thickest wall, pumps to your entire body. Ejection Fraction (EF) measures how well it works.

💡 Normal EF: 55–70% (how much blood squeezed out each beat)

Your Blood's Journey

1

Body → Right Heart

Used blood returns to the right side.

2

Right Heart → Lungs

Blood drops off CO₂, picks up fresh O₂.

3

Lungs → Left Heart

Oxygen-rich blood returns to the left side — red and ready.

4

Left Heart → Body

The mighty left ventricle squeezes blood through the aorta to every cell in your body.

♻️

The Cycle Repeats

~20 seconds per cycle. 100,000 beats/day. Over a lifetime, ~1 million barrels — enough to fill 200+ train cars.

🔒 The 4 Valves — One-Way Doors

🚪

Tricuspid

3 flaps · Right side

🚪

Pulmonary

Right side → Lungs

🚪

Mitral

2 flaps · Most replaced

🚪

Aortic

Left side → Body

⚡ Valve problems: Leaky valve = blood flows backward (regurgitation). Sticky valve = blood can't get through (stenosis). Both make the heart work harder.

🛡️ Prevention Guide

The AHA's Life's Essential 8 — 80% of heart disease is preventable.

✓
🥗 Diet
  • 5+ fruit/veg daily
  • Fish 2×/week
  • Salt <2,300mg/day
  • Whole grains
  • Limit added sugars
✓
🏃 Exercise
  • 150 min/week moderate
  • OR 75 min vigorous
  • 2×/week strength
  • Sit less, move more
  • 10-min walks count!
✓
🚭 No Smoking
  • #1 preventable cause
  • Risk down 50% in 1 year
  • Vaping also unsafe
  • Ask for quit meds
✓
😴 Sleep 7-9 hrs
  • Consistent schedule
  • Screen for sleep apnea
  • Poor sleep = 48% higher risk
✓
⚖️ Healthy Weight
  • BMI 18.5-24.9
  • Waist <40" M, <35" F
  • Lose 5-10% = big benefit
✓
🩸 Cholesterol
  • Total <200 mg/dL
  • LDL <100 mg/dL
  • HDL >40 M, >50 F
  • TG <150 mg/dL
✓
📊 Blood Sugar
  • Fasting <100 mg/dL
  • A1c <5.7%
  • Diabetes = 2× MI risk
  • Prediabetes reversible
✓
💙 Blood Pressure
  • Optimal: <120/80
  • High: ≥130/80
  • Stage 2: ≥140/90
  • Often no symptoms!

⚡ Call 911 if:

  • 🔴Chest pain/pressure lasting >5 min, especially with sweating, nausea, or shortness of breath
  • 🔴Pain in jaw, neck, back, or left arm
  • 🔴Severe shortness of breath at rest
  • 🔴Fainting or sudden confusion
  • 🔴Very fast/irregular heartbeat with dizziness

📞 See a doctor soon if:

  • 🟡Mild chest discomfort with activity
  • 🟡Leg/ankle swelling that doesn't improve
  • 🟡Getting tired much easier than usual
  • 🟡Heart palpitations (fluttering, skipping)
  • 🟡Family history of heart disease — start screening by age 40

📋 Understanding Your Tests

Your doctor ordered a heart test — what does it mean? Click any test to learn more.

Sticky patches detect your heart's electrical signals and print them as squiggly lines — your heart's "electrical fingerprint." Takes 5-10 minutes. Completely painless.

🔍 Tells your doctor:

  • • Heart rhythm (regular? too fast/slow?)
  • • Signs of past or current heart attack
  • • Heart muscle thickening

💡 Good to know:

  • • Normal ECG doesn't rule out all problems
  • • May need other tests (stress test, echo)
  • • No prep needed — wear easy-to-remove top

A wand with gel creates sound waves that bounce off your heart — creating a live video on a screen. Think baby ultrasound, but for your heart. 30-60 minutes. Painless.

🔍 Tells your doctor: Ejection Fraction (EF)

  • • How well the heart valves open/close
  • • Size & shape of chambers
  • • Whether all parts squeeze equally
  • • Fluid around the heart

💡 EF is the key number: Normal = 55-70%. Below 40% may mean heart failure (your heart needs help pumping). No radiation, no needles.

Walk on a treadmill with ECG patches as speed and incline increase. Sometimes medication simulates exercise instead. Monitored constantly. Takes about 1 hour total.

📋 Prepare:

  • • Don't eat 2-3 hours before (water OK)
  • • Wear comfy clothes & walking shoes
  • • May need to stop certain meds (ask doctor)

💡 It's safe — serious complications ~1 in 2,500. Goal is to raise heart rate, not run a marathon. You can stop anytime.

A thin tube is inserted through your wrist or groin and guided to your heart arteries. Contrast dye is injected, X-rays show exactly where blockages are. If found, a stent (mesh tube) may be placed right then to open the blockage. 30-90 minutes. Usually stay a few hours after.

⚠️ After the test:

  • • Mild bruising is normal
  • • Drink lots of fluids to flush the dye
  • • Call doctor if: bleeding won't stop, increasing pain, swelling at site

💊 Common Heart Medications

What each one does, why it's prescribed, and what to watch for.

⚠️ Educational only. Never stop or change medication without talking to your doctor.

ACE-I

ACE Inhibitors

Lisinopril, Ramipril, Enalapril

What: Relax blood vessels, lower BP, protect the heart.

Why: High BP, heart failure, post-heart attack, diabetes.

Watch for: Dry cough (common), rarely facial swelling (emergency — call 911).

💡 Take at same time daily. Don't add potassium.

ARB

ARBs

Losartan, Valsartan

What: Same benefits as ACE inhibitors, different mechanism.

Why: High BP, heart failure, kidney protection.

Watch for: Elevated potassium. Usually less cough than ACE inhibitors.

BB

Beta-Blockers

Metoprolol, Bisoprolol, Carvedilol

What: Slow heart rate, reduce strain.

Why: High BP, heart failure, post-heart attack, irregular rhythms.

Watch for: Fatigue, cold hands, vivid dreams, slow pulse.

⚠️ NEVER stop suddenly — must taper.

Statin

Statins

Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin

What: Lower cholesterol AND stabilize artery plaque to prevent heart attacks.

Why: High cholesterol, heart attack prevention, post-stent.

Watch for: Muscle aches (tell your doctor — they may switch).

💡 Take in evening (liver makes more cholesterol at night).

💉

Blood Thinners

Warfarin, Apixaban, Rivaroxaban

What: Prevent blood clots. Don't dissolve existing ones.

Why: AFib (stroke prevention), DVT/PE, mechanical valves.

Watch for: Increased bleeding — nosebleeds, bruising, blood in stool/urine.

💡 Same time daily. Warfarin needs INR tests. Keep green veggies intake consistent.

CCB

Calcium Channel Blockers

Amlodipine, Diltiazem, Verapamil

What: Relax blood vessel walls, lower BP.

Why: High BP, angina, some irregular rhythms.

Watch for: Swollen ankles with amlodipine, headache, flushing. Non-DHP types (diltiazem/verapamil) also slow heart rate.

⚠️ Tell your doctor about ALL drugs — many interactions.

SGLT2

SGLT2 Inhibitors

Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin

What: Originally diabetes drugs — now breakthrough heart failure treatments.

Why: Heart failure (works with AND without diabetes), kidney protection.

Watch for: Genital yeast infections (common), increased urination.

💡 Breakthrough — reduces HF hospitalizations by 30%.

AP

Antiplatelets

Aspirin, Clopidogrel (Plavix)

What: Make platelets less sticky — prevent artery clots.

Why: Post-heart attack, post-stent, stroke prevention.

Watch for: Easy bruising, nosebleeds, stomach upset.

⚠️ Don't stop clopidogrel without your doctor.
