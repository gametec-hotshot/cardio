Thrombotic Disease

Deep Vein Thrombosis

Thrombus formation in deep venous system, most commonly lower extremities. Major risk factor for pulmonary embolism. Requires anticoagulation.

Prevalence

Common

Severity

Moderate

Incidence

1-3 per 1,000 adults annually

Mortality

Low with treatment, high if leads to PE

Common Symptoms

leg swelling leg pain warmth erythema tenderness

Risk Factors

immobility surgery cancer pregnancy oral contraceptives inherited thrombophilia severe infection

Diagnostic Tests

duplex ultrasound d dimer ct venography mri venography

Treatment Options

anticoagulation compression therapy thrombolysis catheter directed therapy
