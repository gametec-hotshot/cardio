Cardiomyopathy

Dilated Cardiomyopathy

Disease of heart muscle with chamber enlargement and impaired systolic function. Characterized by increased ventricular volumes and reduced ejection fraction (<40%). Major cause of heart failure.

Prevalence

Uncommon

Severity

High

Incidence

1 in 2,500 adults

Mortality

Variable, 5-10% annual mortality without treatment

Common Symptoms

dyspnea fatigue edema palpitations orthopnea paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea

Risk Factors

genetic viral infection alcohol hypertension chemotherapy myocarditis

Diagnostic Tests

echocardiogram ecg cardiac mri cardiac biomarkers genetic testing biopsy

Treatment Options

ace inhibitors beta blockers mra sglt2 inhibitors icd lvad heart transplant
