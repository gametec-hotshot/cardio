### 1. **Dilated Cardiomyopathy** ✅
**File:** `/disorders/dilated-cardiomyopathy.html` (264 lines)

**Overview Section:**
- Definition & epidemiology (1-4% prevalence)
- Genetic factors (mutations in titin TTN, myosin MYH7, troponin T)
- Myocardial injury mechanisms (viral, toxic, autoimmune)
- Neurohormonal activation (RAAS, SNS)
- Ventricular remodeling pathophysiology

**Clinical Presentation Section:**
- 6 major symptoms detailed (dyspnea, fatigue, palpitations, edema, chest pain, syncope)
- Physical examination findings (PMI displacement, S3 gallop, murmurs)
- Pulmonary & systemic findings (crackles, JVD, hepatomegaly, peripheral edema)

**Diagnosis Section:**
- Echocardiography (cornerstone): LV dilation, reduced EF <40%, valvular regurgitation
- Cardiac MRI (tissue characterization): myocarditis, fibrosis, iron overload
- Genetic testing: mutation identification for prognosis & family screening
- Endomyocardial biopsy: myocarditis, amyloidosis, sarcoidosis diagnosis
- Additional modalities: ECG, CXR, BNP/NT-proBNP, cardiac catheterization

**Treatment Section (Comprehensive GDMT):**
- ACE-I/ARB/ARNI: valsartan, enalapril, sacubitril/valsartan
- Beta-blockers: carvedilol, metoprolol, bisoprolol
- MRAs: spironolactone, eplerenone with potassium monitoring
- SGLT2 inhibitors: dapagliflozin, empagliflozin
- Device therapy: ICD (EF ≤35%), CRT (QRS ≥120ms), LVAD (bridge/destination)
- Heart transplantation: definitive treatment for end-stage DCM

**Prognosis Section:**
- Overall survival: 25-50% 5-year mortality untreated; improves 50-60% with GDMT
- Risk factors: severe LV dysfunction (EF <20%), elevated biomarkers (BNP >500), myocardial fibrosis
- Favorable factors: young age, HFpEF phenotype, treatment response
- Transplant outcomes: 80-90% 1-year, 60-70% 5-year, 40-60% 10-year survival
- Monitoring: regular clinic visits, echocardiography, biomarker assessment

### 2. **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy** ✅
**File:** `/disorders/hypertrophic-cardiomyopathy.html` (240+ lines)

**Overview Section:**
- Definition: genetic disease with unexplained LV hypertrophy
- Epidemiology: ~1 in 500 (0.2%), most common inherited cardiac disease
- Genetic basis: MYH7 (β-myosin heavy chain), MYBPC3 (myosin-binding protein C)
- Pathophysiology: hyper-contractile state, autosomal dominant inheritance
- Prevalence: 25-30% with LVOT obstruction, 75-80% without

**Clinical Presentation Section:**
- Symptoms: exertional dyspnea, angina, syncope, palpitations
- Physical exam: forceful apical impulse, double apical impulse, S4 gallop
- Systolic murmur characteristics: crescendo-decrescendo at left sternal border
- Carotid pulse: brisk early peak with rapid decline (jerky)
- Bedside maneuvers: Valsalva increases murmur, squatting decreases murmur

**Diagnosis Section:**
- Echocardiography: LV wall thickness ≥15mm (≥13mm with family history)
- Pattern: asymmetric hypertrophy, SAM of mitral valve, LVOT obstruction
- CMR: myocardial fibrosis detection (LGE), apical/lateral wall assessment
- ECG: T-wave inversions, deep Q waves, LVH criteria
- Stress echo: LVOT gradient response to exercise
- Genetic testing: MYH7, MYBPC3 mutations for prognosis

**Treatment Section:**
- First-line: beta-blockers (carvedilol, metoprolol)
- Alternative: non-DHP calcium channel blockers (verapamil, diltiazem)
- Refractory LVOT: disopyramide, myosin inhibitors (mavacamten)
- Invasive: surgical septal myectomy (gold standard), alcohol septal ablation
- ICD implantation: for high-risk sudden death prevention
- Activity restriction: avoid strenuous exercise and competitive sports

**Prognosis Section:**
- Overall: ~1.5% annual mortality in asymptomatic; 2-4% in symptomatic
- Untreated high-risk: 5-10% annual sudden death rate
- With ICD: <1% annual sudden death rate
- Major SCD risk factors: wall thickness >30mm, unexplained syncope, NSVT, abnormal BP response
- Long-term complications: atrial fibrillation (20-25%), heart failure (5-10%), stroke/TIA
- Risk stratification: low/intermediate/high risk with corresponding management

- Definition and epidemiology

- Physical examination findings

- Bedside maneuvers and special signs

### 3. **Diagnosis**
- Primary imaging modality (Echo)
- Advanced imaging (CMR/CT)
- Electrocardiographic findings

### 4. **Treatment**
- Pharmacological therapy (first-line, alternatives, refractory)
- Interventional procedures (catheter-based)
- Surgical interventions
- Device therapy (ICD, CRT, LVAD)
- Supportive measures and lifestyle modification

### 5. **Prognosis**
- Survival outcomes (short/mid/long-term)
- Risk stratification and prognostic factors
- Complications and natural history
- Impact of treatment on outcomes
- Monitoring and follow-up recommendations
