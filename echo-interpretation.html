Echocardiography Interpretation Guide

Standard Echocardiographic Views

  • Parasternal Long Axis (PLAX): Visualizes LV, LA, aortic valve, mitral valve, aortic root.
  • Parasternal Short Axis (PSAX): Circular view of LV at different levels (mitral valve, papillary muscles, apex).
  • Apical 4-Chamber (A4C): Shows all 4 chambers, tricuspid and mitral valves.
  • Apical 2-Chamber (A2C): LV and LA, mitral valve.
  • Apical 5-Chamber (A5C): Similar to A4C but with aortic valve.
  • Subcostal 4-Chamber: Alternative to A4C, useful in ventilated patients.
  • Suprasternal Notch: Aortic arch and great vessels.

Common Echocardiographic Findings

  • Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH): IVS or PW >1.1 cm, increased LV mass.
  • Left Ventricular Systolic Dysfunction: EF <55%, increased LVESD.
  • Mitral Regurgitation: Color Doppler shows regurgitant jet into LA; quantify vena contracta, PISA.
  • Aortic Stenosis: Thickened leaflets, reduced opening; Vmax >4 m/s, mean gradient >40 mmHg.
  • Aortic Regurgitation: Diastolic jet into LV; measure regurgitant volume.
  • Pericardial Effusion: Anechoic space around heart; assess for tamponade (RA/RV collapse).
  • Cardiac Tamponade: Diastolic collapse of RA/RV, IVC plethora (>2.1 cm, <50% inspiratory collapse).
  • Wall Motion Abnormalities: Regional hypokinesis/akinesis in coronary artery territories.
  • Valvular Vegetation: Mobile echodensities on valves in endocarditis.
  • Thrombus: Echogenic masses in LA appendage or LV apex.

Note: Always correlate with clinical context and consider 3D/strain imaging for advanced assessment.
